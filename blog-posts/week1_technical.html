All things Amazonian

I'm not particularly enamored with doing this blog critique, to be certain. It's probably because I feel wholly under capacity when it comes to making my own websites, and rather exhausted here at the end of the week.

Still, on we go.

Like others, Amazon, to me, is pretty awesome. But, I'll tell you one thing that really chaps my hide about it, one very specific thing.

I was just the other day saving some supplies to a wish list that I hope to purchase and use to trick my van (or is it pimp my ride?) with a solar system. I already have built in a flip down king sized sleeper, and hot shower off of the back (yes, I know, I'm crushing camping, yay me). So, anyway, to get to the point, when you roll over the Wish List button it has a long delay before it populates the drop down. I like to use the drop down because it includes the specific personalized wish lists. Well, it takes so long to populate that I just have been clicking on Wish List, thinking that would make it open the drop down, but, instead it pushes the item to my generic Wish List, BOO!

I do love Amazon, though, and am not sure exactly how I might wean myself from them, given their nasty management policies of their workers.

Facebook, the killer of happiness

Just today on the beautiful, shiney, silly, depressed, streamlined Facefeed, I saw a rather awesome link in a comment. The OP (original post) was a nasty rumored article suggestign that FB might actually have taken part in creating an experiment to see if they might make certain people unhappy... or was it depressed. Either way, 'twas clickbait, and babyJesus knows I've no time for that Tom Foolery, but still, I couldn't help but love the comment. Some chucklehead posted an Onion article, entitled (or is it titled?) "Facebook teens leave in droves to the comments section of a deer fighting human video". I know. I know. But what else is there to say?

The app is clean, the rollovers are responsive, but you know, I still feel like I'm trapped in a socially ungifted persons maze-fuck of unhappy disrepair. Someone poke me... or not.
